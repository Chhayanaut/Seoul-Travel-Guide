Seoul Healing Course

It is a course for people who want to relax and feel nature in Seoul.

(a.m) gilsangsa temple -> Han Sang-soo Embroidery Museum -> Korea Furniture Museum

gilsangsa temple

Located inside the alley along the northeast residential area, it is a special temple where you can get peace in the city center. A temple stay is also in operation, where visitors can participate in a sit-down program at a Buddhist temple that retains traditional Korean beauty.

Han Sang-soo Embroidery Museum

Museums operated by Han Sang-soo, an important intangible cultural festival, for generations. There is also an experience program where you can embroider yourself from the high-quality embroidery exhibition used in the palace.

Korea Furniture Museum

The Korea Furniture Museum is a specialized museum that collects, preserves, and displays 2,500 Korean traditional wooden furniture, and has moved about 10 old Korean houses, allowing you to see the use and lifestyle of our furniture in a real hanok.

Seoul City Wall (Baekak Section)

The four gates connecting the fortress road, where you can enjoy the beautiful view of downtown Sadamun, Gyeongbokgung Palace, and Changdeokgung Palace. It was not until 2007 that it became a civilian access permit section, but it is only accessible through limited information centers at both ends.

Grilled Fish Alley (Dongdaemun)

The restaurant has been in charge of eating at Dongdaemun Market and Pyeonghwa Market since 40 years ago. A place filled with the smell of savory fish baked over briquettes. There are also restaurants specializing in chicken that have become an essential course for traveling to travel to Seoul
